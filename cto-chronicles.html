⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid
Morning! You're up bright and early today.
Ha, yeah. I'm an early bird — always have been. I like to ease into the day before things pick up.
I wish I could do that. I always hit the snooze button like three times.
Honestly, it took me a while too. I just got into the habit of making tea first — no phone, no laptop. It makes a huge difference.
That sounds doable actually. Maybe I should give it a shot.
Definitely worth it. Anyway, I'd better get going — big day ahead!
Morning Daniel. Quick heads-up — the board wants a security audit update by EOD. Can you pull something together?
Morning. Sure, I'm on it. I'll have a draft ready by early afternoon. Should I loop in the legal team, or keep it internal for now?
Keep it tight for now. Let's not open a can of worms before we know what we're dealing with.
Understood. I'll flag anything critical and keep the rest high-level. I'll ping you once it's ready.
Perfect. Appreciate you jumping on this.
Happy to. I'll keep you posted.
Hey! What are you up to this weekend? Any plans?
Honestly, I've been cooped up all week. I need to get out of the city and just breathe.
Same here. What if we head up to the mountains? There's a trail I've been meaning to check out for ages.
That sounds perfect. Overnight or just a day out?
Let's play it by ear — depends on the weather. We could always make a night of it.
Works for me. I'll check the forecast and get back to you tonight. And let's grab a bite on the way up.
I'm already starving just thinking about it. It's a plan!
Alright team, keep this tight — 15 minutes max. Marcus, where are we on the firewall module?
I hit a few roadblocks last week. The third-party API docs are all over the place. About two days behind.
I appreciate you flagging that early. Can you walk me through what's blocking you?
The auth endpoint keeps timing out. None of my approaches hold up under load.
Let's get you unblocked today — I'll reach out to their support directly. Nina, anything on your end that depends on this?
Not urgently, but I'll need it by Thursday for the client demo.
Noted. Marcus — let's touch base after this and work out a realistic timeline. I don't want to overpromise.
You look miles away. What are you reading?
Ha — sorry. This book on deep work. I keep putting it off during the week, so Sunday is the only time I can really sink my teeth into it.
I can never switch off on Sundays. My brain just keeps running.
Same. But I've been making a point of protecting Sunday afternoons. No email, no Slack. Just recharge the batteries.
That's easier said than done with your job.
Honestly, yes. But if I don't draw the line somewhere, the week just swallows everything. I need at least one day to catch my breath.
Fair enough. Maybe I should take a leaf out of your book.
Start small — just one distraction-free hour. You'd be surprised how much it helps.
*Opens notebook* Okay — let me map out the week before it maps me.
You do this every Sunday?
Most Sundays, yeah. I just run through my priorities, flag the things that are time-sensitive, and make sure I'm not going into Monday flying blind.
What's on the radar for this week?
The big one is the investor briefing on Wednesday. I need to get my ducks in a row on that. And I want to carve out time Tuesday for the architecture review — that's been on the back burner too long.
Should I block time on your calendar?
Please do. Two hours, no interruptions. I need to think things through properly before that meeting.
Daniel — security team just flagged something. Looks like someone tried to brute-force the admin panel.
When did this start? Do we know the attack vector?
About two hours ago. They're still looking into it, but it seems like credential stuffing from a leaked database.
Okay, first things first — let's lock down the affected endpoints and loop in the infrastructure team. I don't want us to jump to conclusions, but we need to err on the side of caution.
Already pinged them. Should we bring in the external vendor for a forensic analysis?
Not yet. Let's assess the damage internally first. If it turns out to be a false alarm, we don't want to rack up unnecessary costs. But keep them on standby.
Got it. I'll set up a war room channel so everyone's on the same page.
Perfect. And draft a quick status update for the leadership team — keep it factual, no speculation. We'll circle back in an hour with findings.
Can I be honest with you? I think you sometimes cut to the chase too fast. People need a moment to catch up.
Wow. I didn't expect that. I'll be honest — it took me a second not to take it personally.
I know. But you've asked me to be straight with you, so — food for thought, right?
Yeah. And you're right. I guess I've always been impatient. But I'm trying to come a long way from that.
You have, actually. You're on the right track — just slow down a little.
Thanks. I'll let that sink in and try to be more mindful in tomorrow's session.
That's all anyone can ask. And honestly, you should have asked for this feedback a lot sooner!
Ha — fair point. I'll take it.
Arash, I want to cut to the chase — you've been doing solid work, but I think you have a blind spot around ownership. You deliver, but you don't always drive.
I hear you. I'll be honest — I wasn't expecting that. Can you give me an example?
Sure. The API migration last month — you executed perfectly, but you waited for someone else to flag the risk. A senior engineer should step up and surface that proactively.
That's fair. I think I was being cautious about overstepping. But I can see how that reads as passive.
Exactly. And I want to give you a stretch goal this quarter — lead the platform audit end-to-end. Own it fully.
I'm genuinely excited about that. Though I may need to push back on scope if other priorities shift — is that okay?
Always. Constructive pushback is part of the job. That's ownership too — knowing when to say 'this doesn't fit' and proposing an alternative.
This is really food for thought. Thanks for being direct — I mean that.
You seem on edge. Tough day?
Yeah. We had a retro and someone finally said what everyone's been tiptoeing around for months. The team doesn't feel heard.
That must have been hard to hear.
Honestly? Part of me wanted to get defensive. But I didn't. I just listened. And I think that's the first time I actually did.
So what are you going to do about it?
Change some things. For real this time, not just pay lip service to it. It's overdue.
Before we start — I want this to be a real conversation, not a box-ticking exercise. I want to hear what's actually broken. And I promise I won't shoot the messenger.
Honestly? The scope kept creeping mid-sprint. Three tickets changed after we committed. It kills momentum.
That's on me. I signed off on those changes without thinking about the cost. That won't happen again without a proper trade-off conversation first.
Also — we need clearer acceptance criteria before we start. Half our rework comes from misunderstandings at the beginning.
Agreed. Let's make that a hard gate from now on. No ticket moves to in-progress without agreed criteria. Marco, can you own that process?
Absolutely. I'll draft a template by Friday.
You sound calm. Shouldn't you be freaking out right now?
I've done the work. I know the material. At some point you just trust the process and show up.
What if they ask something you don't know?
Then I say I don't know, and I'll get back to them. That's more impressive than winging it.
You've come a long way. Remember when you went blank presenting to five people?
Don't remind me. Let's just say I learned the hard way that preparation is everything.
Your growth numbers look strong, but I want to understand your burn rate. At this pace, when do you hit the wall?
At current burn, we have 14 months of runway. But that's a conservative estimate — we've been reducing CAC quarter over quarter and we expect to break even on new cohorts by Q3.
What's your biggest risk right now — honestly?
Talent. Scaling the engineering team without breaking the culture we've built. We've got a handle on the technical side — the human side is harder.
I appreciate the candour. Most founders dance around that question.
There's no point. You'd find it in due diligence anyway.
You look tired. Did you sleep at all?
Not really. I kept thinking about this pile of issues we've been avoiding for months.
Work stuff?
Yeah. It's like… you know when your apartment gets messy and you keep putting it off because you don't know where to start?
That sounds exactly like our garage.
Exactly. Except in my case it's our codebase, and if we don't tackle it soon, new features are going to take twice as long.
So why not just bite the bullet and do it?
Because every time I bring it up, there's always something more urgent. It's never the right moment.
That sounds like an excuse.
Yeah. It probably is.
I pulled the numbers. We're spending about 30% of each sprint just working around legacy code.
Thirty percent. That's not a small tax.
And it's going up. If we kick this down the road another quarter, onboarding new engineers will become a nightmare.
Okay. I'm convinced. What's your proposal — do we carve out a full sprint, or chip away at it alongside features?
I'd say 20% of every sprint, dedicated. That way it doesn't feel like we're grinding to a halt.
Good. Let's call it 'foundation work' internally — not 'tech debt.' That framing actually helps with product.
Smart. I'll draft the scope of work this afternoon.
And Marco — I should have listened to you on this six months ago.
